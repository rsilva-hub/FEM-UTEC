ADVANCED NONLINEAR SIMULATION OF COMPOSITE STRUCTURES

A theoretical and practical course aimed at advanced training in nonlinear simulation using the finite element method, applied to the analysis of special structures and complex structural systems. The program combines theoretical foundations with guided practical exercises, addressing materials such as reinforced concrete, steel, and composite structures through computational simulation.

Start date: to be defined

ADVANCED TRAINING AND BROAD ACCESS

The course is intended for master’s and doctoral students, as well as early-career researchers and professionals, mainly from Civil Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, Mechatronics Engineering, Industrial Engineering, Bioengineering, and related disciplines, interested in deepening their knowledge of nonlinear simulation and advanced numerical methods applied to structural analysis.

Participants are expected to have diverse academic backgrounds, with foundations in mechanics, structural analysis or computational modeling, and to be able to exchange experiences from different technical perspectives.

The program is open to participants from different regions and institutional contexts, promoting an inclusive and high-level academic environment.

INTEGRATED METHODOLOGICAL APPROACH

The course adopts a methodological approach that integrates mathematical modeling, computational mechanics and advanced structural simulation, with the objective of providing a comprehensive understanding of the behavior of complex structural systems.

Throughout the program, finite element method techniques, nonlinear analysis and modeling strategies applied to special structures and complex materials are addressed, combining theoretical foundations with criteria from professional practice and academic research.

The sessions will be led by researchers with extensive experience and will focus on applying the content to representative problems in structural engineering.

PRACTICAL APPLICATION AND COMPUTATIONAL SIMULATION

Participants will develop applied exercises focused on the numerical simulation of nonlinear structural systems, putting into practice the concepts and methods presented throughout the course.

Work will be carried out through guided practical cases, using specialized computational tools and integrating theoretical analysis with the technical interpretation of numerical results.

The course will be delivered in person in a multilingual format (Spanish, English and Portuguese) and will be oriented toward supervised hands-on learning led by specialized instructors.
